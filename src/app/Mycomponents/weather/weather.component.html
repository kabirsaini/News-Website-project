El Nino likely to end by May,Indian
monsoon may be spared

El Nino and La Nina are the two alternating phases of sea-surface temperatures in the eastern Pacific Ocean, off the South American coast, in which the waters get unusually warm or cold. These large-scale alternating phases impact weather events across the globe. The India Meteorological Department (IMD) recently said that both the maximum as well as minimum temperatures are expected to remain above normal in most parts of the country till May.The current spell of ongoing El Nino event in the Pacific Ocean is expected to get completely over in the next couple of months, minimising chances of a hit on the earlier part of the Indian monsoon this year. The latest monthly outlook on the El Nino Southern Oscillation (ENSO) system by the Climate Prediction Centre of the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA) of the United States says ENSO-neutral conditions are expected to prevail between April and June and a La Nina likely to emerge thereafter.
